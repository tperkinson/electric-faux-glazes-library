Bates Clear With Soda Ash Sprinkle

A cone 6 oxidation clear base that becomes more salt- and soda-like when soda ash is sprinkled onto the wet glaze to force localized movement.

Cone 6 soda-fired, salt-fired Researched

Process

Application: Dip the piece in the base glaze, then sprinkle soda ash onto selected wet areas.

Notes: Antonio Martinez says the glaze is typically fired to cone 6 in oxidation. He uses the soda ash addition specifically to make selected areas run and create more surface variety. The source page also shows an 8% Mason Stain 6376 Robin's Egg addition.

Fit: Contains barium carbonate. The soda ash treatment increases movement, so keep it away from foot rings and test over a catch tile.

Notes

This is a strong faux-salt candidate because the sodium is added at the surface rather than buried entirely in the glaze slurry.

  • The clear base gives the soda ash room to announce itself.
  • The treatment should work best where you want selective runs, halos, or more atmospheric edges.
  • It belongs in a test grid next to soda sprays and sodium-heavy shinos, not only next to ordinary clear glazes.
