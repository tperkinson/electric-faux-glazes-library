Flashing Wood Ash

A directional electric wood-ash glaze system designed to mimic flashing, shell halos, and side-fired ash movement in cone 6 oxidation.

Cone 6 wood-fired, flashing, ash-run Researched

Process

Application: Spray from one direction onto pots mounted on shells and wadding.

Notes: Use unwashed wood ash but sieve it to a manageable particle size. Ryan Coppage notes that shells and wadding help block and redirect the spray path and can create shell flashing. Wear gloves and avoid breathing ash dust.

Fit: Ash chemistry varies by source. Test on your clay body and expect movement.

Notes

This is one of the clearest electric-kiln paths toward a wood-fire-like surface because it does not only chase chemistry, it also imitates directional deposition.

  • Spray the glaze from one side rather than coating evenly.
  • Set pots on shells and wadding so the sprayed ash glaze is blocked and redirected.
  • Expect orange blush halos where the glaze line ends because the small soda ash addition can volatilize enough sodium to mark the bare clay.

The article source also emphasizes that you can tune melt with small silica and Gerstley borate adjustments as your ash source changes.
