Recipe

Frasca Ash

A cone 6 wood-ash glaze used by Hall-Hough over Green Dragon Matte, with copper and cobalt variants for green and blue wood-fire-like runs.

Cone 6 wood-fired, ash-run, natural ash Researched

Process

Application: Use over Green Dragon Matte for the published layered result.

Notes: For green, add 4% copper carbonate. For blue, add 2% cobalt carbonate. Hall-Hough side-fired the work on shells and wadding over a drip tray.

Fit: This is a runny ash glaze. Use shells, wadding, or a drip tray.

Notes

Frasca Ash sits on the more literal end of the faux-woodfire spectrum because it actually uses a heavy wood-ash load rather than only imitating the look with iron and clay.

  • The published green example is Frasca Ash over Green Dragon Matte, paired with Ashleighs Rivulet.
  • The copper and cobalt variants make this recipe useful as a family rather than a single glaze.
  • This belongs in the same test series as Flashing Wood Ash, but with a heavier focus on overlaying and runoff.
