Visual Arts Center Shino

A cone 6 oxidation shino base built around soda ash, crawling, and a high-kaolin surface so electric firings can approximate some soda- and salt-adjacent atmospheric behavior.

Cone 6 shino, soda-fired, salt-fired Researched

Process

Application: Dip once or twice for controlled crawling. For a more flashing result, spray a red-iron-loaded variant over the base.

Notes: Ryan Coppage notes that thicker applications increase crawling until the glaze begins to peel back too aggressively. The article describes a white base plus a 4% red-iron version sprayed on top and fired fast to cone 6 to emulate a flashing reduction-shino surface.

Fit: The source published this recipe as a small embedded image. The spodumene percentage here was inferred from the printed 100% total because OCR obscured that numeral. Test before treating the chemistry as fully locked.

Notes

This is the clearest salt/soda-adjacent DIY chemistry in the current hunt because the sodium is already in the recipe at a meaningful level.

  • One or two dips appear to be the useful working zone.
  • Triple-dip crawling looks more like glaze failure than controlled atmospheric emulation.
  • The optional iron-sprayed variant is probably the better branch for flashing and blushed electric salt-look tests.
