Use Notes

Application: Coyote says Oxblood must be applied very thick to turn red; thinner spots stay clear or grayish. The page marks it dinnerware safe and non-toxic, while still advising users to test all foodware.

Food safe: Yes

Sources: coyote-oxblood

Notes

Coyote Oxblood is one of the most literal off-the-shelf faux-reduction glazes in the archive.

  • It is useful when the goal is specifically copper-red behavior without switching to a reduction firing.
  • Thickness is the main process lever, so it belongs in any reduction-look test grid with a strict thin-medium-thick comparison.
  • The record now includes two artist work examples from the official Coyote gallery instead of only the comparison tile.
