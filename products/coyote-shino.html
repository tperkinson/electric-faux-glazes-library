Coyote Shino

A cone 6 commercial shino marketed as giving a gas-fired look in any kiln, with brown breaking over texture and good layering behavior.

Coyote Clay and Color Shino Glazes Cone 6

Effect

Finish tags: brown-break, shino-like, stable

Effect tags: reduction-look, gas-fired look, layerable

Atmosphere: electric

Use Notes

Application: Coyote describes this glaze as easy to apply, not runny, and best over texture or other glazes. The page also notes that crystals can form in liquid glaze if it is stored below 65 F.

Food safe: Yes

Sources: coyote-shino

Notes

This is a strong commercial reduction-look glaze because it targets the brown-breaking shino lane directly rather than only offering generic variegation.

  • It should be tested on carved and ribbed surfaces because the manufacturer explicitly calls out texture response.
  • It is also a good bridge product between the DIY electric shino recipes and commercial brush-on testing.
  • The gallery now includes finished-piece examples from the official Coyote page, not just the glaze tile.
