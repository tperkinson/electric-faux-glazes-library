Test Plan

Cone 6 Shino Comparison Matrix

Cone 6 Planned

Clay Bodies

white cone 6 stoneware, iron-bearing cone 6 stoneware

Comparison Axes

clay body iron content, coat count or dip count, smooth vs textured surface, base shino vs iron-enriched branch, fast electric vs standard electric cycle

Variants

single dip, double dip, triple dip for failure threshold, 4% red-iron-oxide sprayed branch, fast electric cycle

Success Criteria

controlled crawling, convincing shino break, white-to-orange or brown-to-cream variation, no unusable peeling, clear difference between white and iron-bearing clay

Objective

Build a direct shino comparison between the strongest commercial cone 6 shino options and the strongest DIY electric shino recipe already in the archive.

Candidate Set

  • Coyote Shino
  • AMACO Shino
  • Visual Arts Center Shino

Tile Structure

For each candidate:

  1. White stoneware, smooth tile
  2. White stoneware, textured tile
  3. Iron-bearing stoneware, smooth tile
  4. Iron-bearing stoneware, textured tile

Application structure:

  • commercial shinos: 1, 2, and 3 coats when relevant
  • Visual Arts Center Shino: one dip, two dips, and a deliberate heavy/triple-dip failure threshold

Priority Experiments

Priority A

  • Visual Arts Center Shino at one and two dips on both bodies
  • Visual Arts Center Shino with the 4% red-iron-oxide sprayed branch
  • Coyote Shino on textured white and iron-bearing clay

Priority B

  • AMACO Shino on the same tile set
  • fast-cycle comparison for Visual Arts Center Shino
  • direct comparison of controlled crawling versus commercial stability

What To Record

  • exact clay body and iron content
  • dip count or brush-coat count
  • crawling severity
  • where the glaze stays white, goes orange, or breaks brown
  • whether texture helps or hurts the shino read
  • whether the surface reads more reduction-shino, commercial shino, or simply brown breaking glaze

Expected Outcomes

Visual Arts Center Shino is likely to win on authentic electric shino behavior.

Coyote Shino is likely to win on usability and repeatability.

AMACO Shino is likely to sit between those two poles and may prove strongest when layering or broader color variation matter more than crawling.
