Test Plan

Cone 6 Reduction-Red Comparison Matrix

Cone 6 Planned

Clay Bodies

white cone 6 stoneware, buff cone 6 stoneware

Comparison Axes

coat count, clay body color, smooth vs lightly textured surface, normal cool vs slow cool, commercial brush-on vs DIY mixed glaze

Variants

natural cooling, slow cooling, thin-medium-thick application, edge break test on ribbed tile

Success Criteria

convincing reduction-red depth, clean edge transition, no muddy brown collapse, limited pinholing, repeatable result across both clay bodies

Objective

Compare the strongest current cone 6 reduction-red candidates in one compact matrix so the archive can separate:

  • copper-red commercial effects
  • iron-red DIY effects
  • surfaces that read genuinely reduction-like from surfaces that only read red

Candidate Set

  • Coyote Oxblood
  • AMACO PC-70 Copper Red
  • Ketchup Red
  • Tomato Red Base

Tile Structure

For each candidate:

  1. White stoneware, smooth tile
  2. White stoneware, lightly ribbed tile
  3. Buff stoneware, smooth tile
  4. Buff stoneware, lightly ribbed tile

Application structure:

  • commercial glazes: thin, medium, thick
  • DIY glazes: standard, heavy, very heavy

Priority Experiments

Priority A

  • Coyote Oxblood at thick application on white and buff bodies
  • PC-70 Copper Red at the manufacturer's three-coat target on white and buff bodies
  • Tomato Red Base at standard and heavy application on white and buff bodies

Priority B

  • Ketchup Red on the same tile set as Tomato Red Base
  • slow-cool branch for the two DIY reds
  • edge-break comparison on ribbed tiles for all four candidates

What To Record

  • exact clay body
  • exact coat count or wet application weight
  • whether the result reads red, oxblood, brick, brown, or muddy
  • edge color where the glaze thins
  • pinholes, crawling, or blistering
  • whether slow cooling improved or dulled the surface

Expected Outcomes

The likely strongest commercial reduction-red surface is Coyote Oxblood, with PC-70 close behind.

The likely strongest DIY reduction-red analog is Tomato Red Base.

Ketchup Red is the control worth keeping because a simpler matte red may still read more mineral and atmospheric than a glossier, more engineered surface.
