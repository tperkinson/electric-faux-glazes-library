Test Plan

Cone 6 Dark Reduction-Look Matrix

Cone 6 Planned

Clay Bodies

smooth buff cone 6 stoneware, speckled cone 6 stoneware

Comparison Axes

coat count, normal cool vs slow cool, smooth vs textured surface, buff vs speckled clay, commercial oil-spot look vs DIY tenmoku/teadust

Variants

two coats, three coats, normal cooling, slow cooling, textured vertical test tile

Success Criteria

dark reduction-like depth, visible spotting or crystal activity, good texture response, limited bubbling on speckled clay, clear visual distinction between black oil-spot and amber tenmoku lanes

Objective

Compare the two strongest dark cone 6 oxidation candidates that can stand in for reduction-fired oil-spot, tenmoku, and teadust surfaces.

Candidate Set

  • Mayco SW-111 Wrought Iron
  • Tenmoku Gold

Tile Structure

For each candidate:

  1. Buff stoneware, smooth tile
  2. Buff stoneware, textured tile
  3. Speckled stoneware, smooth tile
  4. Speckled stoneware, textured tile

Application structure:

  • Wrought Iron: two and three coats
  • Tenmoku Gold: standard and heavy application

Priority Experiments

Priority A

  • Wrought Iron on buff and speckled clay at two coats
  • Tenmoku Gold on buff clay with normal cooling and slow cooling
  • textured-tile comparison for both candidates

Priority B

  • Wrought Iron three-coat branch
  • Tenmoku Gold heavy-application branch
  • close inspection of bubbling on speckled bodies

What To Record

  • exact clay body
  • coat count or wet thickness
  • cooling profile
  • whether the surface reads black, brown-black, amber, gold, or metallic gray
  • crystal, spot, or mottling development
  • bubbling, blistering, or matte/gloss imbalance

Expected Outcomes

Wrought Iron is likely to be the quickest path to a commercial oil-spot-like surface.

Tenmoku Gold is likely to be richer when cooling is controlled well.

The useful decision point is not which glaze is "better" in the abstract, but whether the target surface is black metallic oil spot or amber-black tenmoku/teadust.
