Test Plan

Cone 6 Woodfire-Look Comparison Matrix

Cone 6 Planned

Clay Bodies

white cone 6 stoneware, buff cone 6 stoneware

Comparison Axes

coat count, clay body color, natural cool vs slow cool, smooth vs textured surface, single glaze vs layered stack

Variants

natural cooling, slow cooling, directional spray application, layered application over matte base

Success Criteria

visible movement, ash-like pooling or streaking, texture break, no unacceptable runoff, strong contrast on white and buff bodies

Objective

Build a compact cone 6 matrix that compares the most promising DIY and commercial candidates for wood-fire-looking electric surfaces.

Ashleighs Rivulet plus Frasca Ash over a matte base

  • V.C. Matte under Hannahs Fake Ash

Commercial Set

  • AMACO Shino
  • Mayco SW-122 Maycoshino
  • Mayco SW218 Micro Ash
  • Coyote Archies Base

Tile Structure

For each candidate or stack:

  1. White stoneware, smooth surface
  2. White stoneware, textured surface
  3. Buff stoneware, smooth surface
  4. Buff stoneware, textured surface

For thickness:

  • commercial brush-on glazes: 1, 2, and 3 coats when relevant
  • DIY glazes: normal, heavy, and layered application

Priority Experiments

Priority A

  • Flashing Wood Ash, directional spray, white and buff clay
  • Maycoshino, 1/2/3 coats on white and buff clay
  • Micro Ash, 2/3 coats on textured white and buff clay

Priority B

  • V.C. Matte plus Hannahs Fake Ash
  • Matte base plus Ashleighs Rivulet and Frasca Ash
  • Archies Base over a dark underlayer or stronger color field

What To Record

  • exact clay body
  • exact coat count or spray weight
  • cooling profile
  • whether shells or wadding were used
  • runoff severity
  • texture break quality
  • whether the result reads more like ash, shino, reduction, or layered commercial glaze

Expected Outcomes

The likely winners are:

  • Flashing Wood Ash for the most literal wood-fire mimicry
  • Hall-Hough layered stack for convincing ash-run surfaces
  • Maycoshino for the easiest commercial shino-adjacent surface
  • Micro Ash for the richest modern ash/crystalline commercial surface
  • Archies Base for slow-cool-sensitive commercial interaction
