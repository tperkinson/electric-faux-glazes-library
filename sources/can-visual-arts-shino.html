Ryan Coppage lays out a useful sodium-forward path toward electric faux salt and soda surfaces by reframing cone 6 oxidation shino as a controlled crawling system rather than a literal reduction substitute.

Useful details from the article:

  • soda ash is called out as a salt that drives spotting, blushes, soda glass, and other surface aesthetics
  • Coppage notes that 4-16% soda ash can create soda-salt crystals on the glaze surface
  • the example cups were fired to cone 6 in an electric kiln, using a white base plus a sprayed red-iron variant for a more flashing result

The article includes a small embedded recipe image for the base shino. The recipe record created from this source notes that the spodumene percentage was inferred from the printed 100% total because OCR obscured that single numeral.
